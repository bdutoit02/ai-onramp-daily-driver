# You are preparing a report for an organisation that provides training resources to banks in South Africa. The report should identify the key skills shortages emerging in the South African banking sector over the next three years, and recommend the training priorities that should shape the organisation's programme development.

Here's the report on South Africa's emerging banking skills shortages and training priorities for 2026–2028. It covers the following key areas:

**Six Priority Skills Shortage Clusters:**

1. **Data Science, Analytics \& AI Engineering** — the deepest structural shortage, with Data Management Manager, Software Developer, and Data Scientist among BANKSETA's top-priority occupations for 2025–2030, and 38% of banks reporting that AI skills scarcity directly slows deployment
2. **Cybersecurity \& Digital Risk** — 63% of cybersecurity roles partially or fully unfilled nationally; SA recorded the highest ransomware detections on the continent in 2024
3. **Risk, Compliance \& Regulatory Management** — Basel IV (live from July 2025), FATF greylisting obligations, and the COFI Bill are creating simultaneous demand spikes for specialised compliance skills
4. **AI Governance \& Model Risk** — an underprovided niche opening up fast following the FSCA/PA's November 2025 AI report and South Africa's emerging national AI Policy
5. **Credit, Financial Analysis \& Quantitative Skills** — Credit Manager appeared in the HTFV top 10 five times over five years; NPL pressures are intensifying demand for advanced credit practitioners
6. **Management, Leadership \& Transformation** — Corporate General Manager carries the largest absolute sector need in BANKSETA's priority occupation list (554 vacancies)

**Key Strategic Recommendations** include building programmes at the intersection of banking domain knowledge and technical skills (the most underserved gap), prioritising CPD-accredited short formats, aligning to BANKSETA PIVOTAL funding criteria, and developing AI governance as a standalone niche with very limited current competition.
